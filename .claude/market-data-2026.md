## The core thesis (use this framing everywhere)

The self-storage market in 2026 splits into winners and losers based on **operations, not location**. REITs (Extra Space, Public Storage, CubeSmart) average 92.3% same-store occupancy. Non-designated independents average 87.2%. The 5–10 point occupancy gap = $72K+ in annual revenue loss per 500-unit facility = ~$1.3M in lost asset value at a 5.5% cap.

The gap is closeable, and the lever is **marketing + revenue management technology** — exactly what StorageAds delivers. This is the central narrative for every customer-facing asset.

**Talking point:** The era of "low rate + aggressive ECRI" is ending. Operators need new customers continuously to replace churn — and that means actual demand-gen marketing. StorageAds is the alternative to the pricing-trick playbook regulators are killing.

### For ECRI / "REIT playbook" educational content

REITs run a 3-part playbook independents mostly don't:
1. **Dynamic street rate pricing** — daily algorithmic adjustments
2. **Existing Customer Rate Increases (ECRIs)** — 90–180 day cycles, 8–15% (sometimes 20%+) per cycle
3. **Achieved rate management** — gap between new-customer and long-term-customer rates

At peak, ECRIs drove in-place rents 40%+ above street rates. Spread collapsed to 3.7% above street rates as of Q3 2025, then fell 3.1% YoY. Q4 2025 spread widened back to **18.9%**.

Independents who run systematic ECRI programs close **3–5 points** of the occupancy-equivalent revenue gap without adding a single unit. StorageAds doesn't directly run ECRIs, but our messaging should acknowledge this lever exists and pair it with our demand-gen value.
