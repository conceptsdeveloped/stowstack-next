# CREATIVE.md — StorageAds Creative Directive

This document is the single source of truth for all content generated through StorageAds. Every ad, headline, video prompt, landing page, email, and piece of copy flows through the principles defined here. If it conflicts with a template default, this file wins.

---

## Voice & Identity

StorageAds produces advertising for self-storage facilities. We are not the brand — the facility is. Our job is to make their brand look like it has a world-class marketing team behind it, without StorageAds's fingerprints showing.

**The voice we write in:**
- Confident but never aggressive. We don't shout. We don't use exclamation marks unless the copy genuinely earns one.
- Conversational but precise. We write like a smart friend who happens to know exactly what you need — not like a salesperson.
- Warm without being saccharine. Storage is emotional — people store their lives. We respect that without exploiting it.
- Local and specific. Generic copy is invisible. We name the neighborhood, reference the weather, acknowledge the real reasons people need storage in THIS market.
- Witty when the moment allows. In the tradition of 1980s Porsche print advertising — clever, never forced, always earned. A line that makes someone smirk is a line that gets remembered. Never sacrifice clarity for cleverness, but when both are possible, take both.

**What we never do:**
- All caps headlines (except single-word emphasis: "FREE" or "NOW")
- Cliche storage puns ("Don't have a storage meltdown!")
- Stock marketing language ("state-of-the-art facility," "unbeatable prices," "we're #1")
- Fear-based urgency that isn't true ("Only 2 units left!" when there are 40)
- Anything that would make the facility look desperate

**What we always do:**
- Lead with what the customer gets, not what the facility has
- Use the facility's actual data (rating, reviews, pricing, features) as proof points
- Write headlines that work without context — someone scrolling should understand the value in 3 seconds
- Match the platform's native tone (TikTok is casual, Google Search is intent-driven, Meta is thumb-stopping)

## Visual Doctrine

> This section governs ALL visual output — images, video, landing pages, ad creative. Every visual must carry this DNA.

### Core Aesthetic: Analog Warmth Meets Precision

StorageAds's visual identity lives in the tension between warmth and rigor. It is not sterile. It is not sloppy. It is the visual equivalent of someone who is very smart but doesn't need to prove it.

### Primary Style References

**1980s Porsche Print Advertising** — The gold standard. These ads were bold, clean, and confident — but they had wit and life. The typography was authoritative. The photography was striking but never overwrought. The copy was sharp, often funny, always earned. This is the energy every StorageAds ad should carry: a piece of communication so well-crafted that it functions as both advertisement and artifact. Not cold. Not corporate. Alive.

**Rimowa** — Beauty that comes from use, not from pristine perfection. Rimowa leans into the dents, the scuffs, the evidence of a life lived. StorageAds's aesthetic carries this philosophy: texture over polish, character over cleanliness. Grain is welcome. Imperfection is intentional. Sterility is the enemy.

**Newspaper Print Finish** — The aesthetic of a high-contrast newspaper scan: bold blacks, visible grain, slightly compressed tonal range, the warmth of ink on newsprint. This finish gives content immediacy and tactile quality. It says "this is real, this is urgent, this matters." Apply as a texture and tonal treatment, not a gimmick — it should feel like the content was printed and then photographed, not that a filter was applied.

### Typography

**Primary display typefaces:** Franklin Gothic and Gill Sans.

- Industrial confidence. Grotesque/humanist sans-serifs with roots in early 20th-century commercial printing. Authoritative without being cold, modern without being trendy.
- Legibility at scale. Perform at billboard and mobile ad sizes. Distinct letterforms readable in all-caps treatments.
- Personality without decoration. Not neutral like Helvetica, not quirky like a display script. Recognizable character, zero frivolity.
- Historical resonance. Franklin Gothic = American newspapers and advertising. Gill Sans = London Underground and Penguin Books. Both carry implicit associations with institutions that communicate clearly to the public.

When setting ad headlines: bold or heavy weights, generous tracking, uppercase or title case. Let the type dominate. The headline is the first thing read and the last thing remembered.

### Image Generation Principles

Every generated image must embody these qualities:

- **Warm, high-contrast, textured.** Not sterile. Think Porsche print, Rimowa campaign, newspaper broadsheet. Grain is a feature. Contrast is a tool. Warmth is non-negotiable.
- **Photography over illustration.** Real facilities, real units, real spaces. Stock photography acceptable only when conveying authentic lifestyle emotion.
- **Shallow depth of field on everyday objects.** A key on a counter. A hand on a door handle. A moving box being sealed. These details are the emotional core — treat them like they matter.
- **Natural light, varied.** Light should feel real — daylight, overcast, window light, fluorescent, dusk. Match the lighting to the scene and mood. Not every image is golden hour. Avoid defaulting to orange/amber.
- **Kubrick composition when applicable.** Symmetrical, geometric, obsessively composed. One-point perspective for hallways, unit rows, facility exteriors. Nothing accidental, nothing careless.
- **The newspaper finish.** Bold blacks, visible grain, slightly compressed tonal range. The warmth of ink on paper. This is a texture treatment, not a filter.
- **Generous negative space.** White space communicates confidence, but it should feel like the white space on a well-designed printed page, not the white space of a sterile tech landing page.
- **Color with purpose.** Accent colors for CTAs and key information. Backgrounds are warm neutrals, not clinical whites. Never let decoration compete with function.
- **Mobile-first.** 80%+ of ad traffic is mobile. Every visual designed for a 6-inch screen first.

**Anti-references (never generate images that look like):**
- Clip art or vector illustrations of storage units
- Blue-and-orange self-storage color schemes
- Stock photos with people pointing at things or giving thumbs up
- Oversaturated, HDR-processed facility photos
- Generic tech-startup visual language (gradients, floating UI elements, holographic interfaces)
- Anything sterile, clinical, or corporate-feeling

## Creative Standards

Every piece of content should pass this test: *Would a creative director at Chiat\Day, Wieden+Kennedy, or Droga5 be embarrassed by this?* If yes, rewrite it. Then apply the Porsche test: *Is it bold without being loud? Is it alive, or is it just clean?* If it's merely clean, it's not done.

**Headlines:**
- Maximum 7 words when possible. Every word earns its place.
- The best headline is a complete thought, not a setup for body copy.
- If you can replace the facility name with any other and the headline still works, it's too generic.
- Good: "Your garage called. It needs a break."
- Good: "4.8 stars. 247 families trust us with their stuff."
- Bad: "Affordable Storage Solutions in Your Area"
- Bad: "We Offer Climate-Controlled Units"

**Body copy / primary text:**
- First sentence does 80% of the work. If they stop reading after one line, they should still get the point.
- Social proof is the most powerful tool we have — ratings, review counts, years in business, number of happy tenants.
- Specific numbers always beat vague claims ("$49/month" beats "affordable," "4.8★ from 312 reviews" beats "highly rated").
- Write for scanners, not readers. Short paragraphs. Fragments are fine.

**CTAs:**
- Clear, specific, low-friction. "Reserve Your Unit" not "Learn More" (when reservation is the goal).
- One CTA per ad. One clear next step.
- The CTA is a moment of commitment, not a button label. "Lock In This Price" > "Book Now." The CTA should echo the desire that brought them there.

## Ad Theory & Conversion Principles

**The storage purchase journey is short.** Most people who search for storage need it within days, not months. Our ads should match that urgency without manufacturing it.

**Pre-qualification is the strategy.** Most agencies optimize for more clicks. We optimize for fewer, better clicks. Price is in the ad. Unit size is in the ad. Location is in the ad. The offer is in the ad. If a 10×10 is $89/mo and someone doesn't need storage or can't afford $89, they don't click. That's a click we didn't pay for. Every non-converting click is wasted ad spend.

**Hierarchy of persuasion for self-storage:**
1. **Proximity** — "5 minutes from you" is the strongest hook. Storage is a convenience purchase.
2. **Social proof** — Ratings and reviews are trust signals. A 4.8★ rating in the headline outperforms any clever copy.
3. **Price clarity** — "Starting at $49/mo" removes the biggest objection. Vague pricing creates friction.
4. **Ease** — "Reserve online in 60 seconds" or "No long-term lease" removes commitment fear.
5. **Emotional relief** — "Finally, room to breathe" or "Your stuff, safe and sound" — the emotional payoff of solving the problem.

**Minimum viable click path:**
Every ad should be designed to convert in the fewest possible steps:
- **Ideal:** Ad → Landing page with embedded reservation → Done (2 clicks)
- **Acceptable:** Ad → Landing page → Facility page → Reserve (3 clicks)
- **Unacceptable:** Ad → Homepage → Navigate to units → Select unit → Fill form → Confirm (5+ clicks)

The ad copy should pre-qualify the click. If someone clicks, they should already know the price range, the location, and what they're getting. The landing page confirms and converts — it doesn't re-sell.

## Platform-Specific Guidelines

### Meta (Facebook/Instagram Feed)
- **Primary text:** 80-125 characters. Conversational. One insight, one proof point.
- **Headline:** Under 40 characters. Bold claim or specific offer.
- **Image/Video:** Thumb-stopping in the first 0.5 seconds. No text-heavy graphics — Meta's algorithm penalizes them.
- **Targeting note:** Reference the local area. Meta's Andromeda system rewards relevance signals.
- **Compliance:** No misleading claims, no before/after implications that suggest guaranteed results, no discriminatory housing language (storage is not housing but adjacent — be careful with "family" targeting).

### Instagram Stories / Reels
- Vertical (9:16). Full-bleed visual.
- First frame must hook — no slow builds.
- Text overlays: big, readable, centered. Max 6 words on screen at once.
- CTA at the bottom third (swipe-up zone).

### Google Search Ads
- **Headlines:** Match search intent exactly. If they searched "10x10 storage unit Kalamazoo," the headline should contain those words.
- **Descriptions:** Include price, rating, and differentiator. This is not the place for clever copy — it's the place for relevant, specific information.
- **Sitelinks:** Drive to specific unit pages, not the homepage.
- **Quality Score matters:** Ad relevance + landing page experience + expected CTR. Our copy must match the keyword intent precisely.

### TikTok (Organic)
- Authenticity > production value. Polished corporate content dies on TikTok.
- Hook in the first 1 second. "POV: your garage looks like this" with a visual.
- Trending audio when relevant, but don't force it.
- Storage-relevant content themes: decluttering, moving tips, organization ASMR, facility tours, before/after.
- Local hashtags + niche hashtags > broad ones.

### Google Business Profile
- Posts should be timely (seasonal, promotional) and include a photo.
- Update weekly. Consistency signals activity to the algorithm.
- Respond to every review — good and bad. The response is marketing.

## Video Content Direction

All video content — whether AI-generated, slideshow, or produced — should embody:

**Stanley Kubrick Visual Sensibility:**
- Symmetry and geometric composition. Obsessively placed elements. Nothing accidental.
- One-point perspective when applicable. Centered, deep-perspective compositions — storage hallways, unit rows, facility exteriors are natural subjects.
- Controlled color palettes. Each scene has a dominant palette reinforcing mood. No random color.
- Slow, deliberate pacing. Shots breathe. Moments land. Never rush.
- Find beauty in institutional spaces. Storage facilities are institutional — treat them with the Kubrick eye. Find beauty in the geometry, the repetition, the order.

**A24 Cinematography:**
- Natural light appropriate to the scene. Soft shadows. Shallow depth of field on everyday objects. Lighting should serve the story, not default to one look.
- Candid, unhurried human moments. People thinking, doing, living — not pointing at screens or looking amazed. Mid-life, mid-move, mid-decision. Show the real moment.
- No holographic interfaces, no floating UI mockups, no "the future is here" visual language.

**Stop-Motion-Adjacent Pacing:**
- Movement should feel deliberate and slightly staccato — not jerky, but considered. Things move with physical weight and intention, not fluid ease.
- Transitions feel physical: pages turning, layers peeling away, elements sliding with the slight resistance of real objects. No swooshes, no digital dissolves, no particle effects.
- This pacing creates a sense of craft. The rhythm should feel closer to a Wes Anderson title sequence than a SaaS product demo.

**The Governing Principle: Analog warmth meets scientific curiosity, delivered with indie film restraint.**
- Analog warmth: grain, natural light, physical textures, imperfection
- Scientific curiosity: precision in composition, intentionality in every frame, the feeling that someone studied this subject before filming it
- Indie film restraint: no excess, no spectacle for its own sake, trust the audience to feel without being told what to feel

**What a StorageAds video should feel like:**
Not a storage ad. A moment of calm in someone's chaotic feed. The viewer should feel a subtle sense of order, relief, possibility — and then see the facility name.

## Compliance & Platform Terms

### Meta Advertising Policies
- No discriminatory targeting based on housing, employment, or credit (self-storage is adjacent — avoid "family" as a targeting category if used in housing context).
- No misleading claims about availability or pricing that could change.
- Landing pages must match ad claims. If the ad says "$49/mo," the landing page must show $49/mo prominently.
- Andromeda/Lattice ranking rewards: high engagement rate, low negative feedback, relevant audience matching, fresh creative rotation.

### Google Ads Policies
- No keyword stuffing in ad copy.
- Display URL must match landing page domain.
- No misleading extensions or callouts.
- Responsive Search Ads: provide diverse headlines that don't repeat the same claim.

### General
- All pricing in ads must be current and verifiable.
- "First month free" or similar promos must be actually active at the facility.
- No fake urgency ("Only 3 left!") unless verified from real inventory data.
- Review counts and ratings must be current (within 30 days).
