## What's in each

### What every plan includes

Every StorageAds plan, even Good, includes the things every facility should have and almost none do.

- **Your Google Business Profile, worked weekly.** Posts. Reviews answered. Common questions seeded. Photos kept fresh. The single biggest local-search lever almost no operator pulls.
- **Your Facebook and Instagram pages, posted to.** Three posts a week, written for your facility, your weather, your neighborhood.
- **A high-converting website for your facility.** Built by us, your domain or ours, alongside your current site. Switch traffic when you're ready.
- **Lead capture on your site.** Web form. Phone-number click tracking. Tied to a real dashboard.
- **The full dashboard.** Occupancy. Move-ins. What every channel cost. What every channel returned. Apples to apples.
- **Monday-morning email.** What happened last week. What's next.
- **Direct support.** From real operators. Not a ticket queue.

- Three Google Business Profile posts per week

Good is for the facility owner who wants the basics handled and isn't ready for the bigger toolkit yet. It's enough to move the needle on local search and start getting GBP and social working again.

**Do I need to switch my PMS?** No. Keep what you've got. We sit on top.

**Do you take a cut of my ad spend?** No. You pay Meta and Google directly. We manage strategy, creative, and targeting. Your budget, your account, your invoice.

**Who's on the other end if I have a problem?** Real operators. Most of the team has run a facility. You're not going to be in a Zendesk queue.

**Will AI mess up something my tenants see?** We've built guardrails for that — reviews, complaints, and anything sensitive get human eyes before they go out. Our team reads 5-10% of everything else to catch drift early.

**How fast can I be live?** 20 minutes on Good, same day on Better, 24 hours on Best.

## Internal pricing notes (not for the customer-facing page)

**Tier rationale:**

- The $199-799 band lands the entry tier at the no-brainer price point identified in vision doc §6 and stretches the top tier to the full-feature mark without pricing out the beachhead. The premium between Good and Better ($200) is the price of the AI receptionist + lifecycle bundle; that's the single sharpest upsell driver because the operator can directly see "I lost X leads last month from slow response."
- The Best premium ($400 over Better) is for competitor intel + churn prevention + rate recommendations + cross-facility benchmarking. These are the "I want every lever on" features. Conversion will be slower but ACV justifies sales-assisted close.
- Custom tier exists to capture management-company white-label and 4+ facility operators where per-facility pricing doesn't work. The "let's talk" framing is intentional — no published price for whales.

**Risk reversal:** 30-day money-back guarantee selected over free trial and over the bolder occupancy-guarantee framing. Card upfront produces commitment; MBG produces low-friction exit; occupancy guarantee was considered and declined as operationally fragile during the alpha phase. Reconsider an occupancy guarantee for Best tier after Phase 2 produces enough operator-portfolio data to underwrite it credibly.

**Ad spend pass-through:** explicitly NOT passed through StorageAds books. Operator pays Meta and Google directly. This decision sacrifices a margin opportunity (some agencies take 15-20% of ad spend) in exchange for clean unit economics, transparent operator pricing, and zero accounting burden. The trade is right for the beachhead segment that has been burned by agency markups before.

**Annual discount:** 20% off plus one free month is aggressive — implied retention math means the annual operator is paying ~7.2 months for the year vs. the 12 a monthly operator pays. The aggression is justified by cash flow improvement and by the retention signal (annual prepayment is the single strongest leading indicator of long-tenure SaaS customers).

**No setup fee:** decision per vision doc §6. The cost of setup (initial AI generation, GBP OAuth wizard, content calendar generation, LP build) is real but should be amortized into the recurring price rather than friction at the door. For Better and Best the optional one-time professional photo shoot ($299 add-on for Good, included in Better/Best) is the only physical-world cost that surfaces separately.

**Operator language:** the page is in operator voice (`.claude/copy-voice.md`) throughout. The "Common questions" section uses contractions and short answers in Nick-Huber-tweet cadence. The internal-notes block at the bottom is the only section in pitch-adjacent register and should be removed before any customer publication.

**A/B test candidates for after Phase 2 launch:**

- "Free 14-day trial, no card" against "30-day money-back, card upfront" — current default is the card-upfront MBG, but the free-trial framing converts higher in some SaaS comps.
- "Pay annually, get a month free" against "Pay annually, save 20%" — math is similar but the framing converts differently.
- Best tier price testing at $799 vs. $999 — the $999 mark would lift ACV by 25% but may suppress conversion for the solo operator segment.

**Phase 2 pricing-validation gate:** the cost-per-facility-per-month operating profile from the Phase 1 pilot (per `docs/operator-os-pilot-measurement-plan.md` §2.3) determines whether the $199 Good tier is economically viable. If the all-in operating cost per facility (Anthropic, Postiz, Twilio, infrastructure, QA labor) exceeds $80/month at alpha scale, the Good tier price needs to be raised to $249 or the Good tier feature set narrowed. Decision deferred to the Phase 1 cost profile report.
