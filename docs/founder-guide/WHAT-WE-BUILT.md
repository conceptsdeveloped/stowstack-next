# What We Built

*A plain-English tour of the product. No code. The goal is that after reading this, you could explain StorageAds to your dad.*

---

## The one-sentence version

StorageAds is software that runs the entire online marketing operation for a self-storage facility — the ads, the website, the lead follow-up, the Google reviews, the reports — and is built to prove which ad dollars actually turned into a tenant moving in.

Think of it as **hiring a marketing agency, a call-tracking service, a landing-page builder, a CRM, and a data analyst — except it's one piece of software, it never sleeps, and it's built by a storage operator instead of a marketing guy.**

---

## The mental model: a funnel with a sensor at every step

Picture the journey a future tenant takes:

```
  Someone needs storage
          │
   1. Sees your ad         ← StorageAds creates & runs the ad (Meta, Google, TikTok)
          │
   2. Clicks to a page     ← StorageAds builds a landing page for that facility
          │
   3. Becomes a lead       ← StorageAds captures them (form, tracked phone #, or walk-in QR code)
          │
   4. Gets followed up     ← StorageAds sends automated emails/texts until they convert or go cold
          │
   5. Signs a lease &      ← This is the MONEY moment. "Cost per move-in" is measured HERE,
      moves in                not at step 3.
          │
   6. The ad robots learn  ← StorageAds is *supposed* to tell Meta/Google "this exact person
                              moved in" so they go find more people like them.
```

Every marketing tool on the market measures steps 1–3. **The entire reason StorageAds exists is steps 5 and 6** — connecting real money (a move-in) back to the ad that caused it, then teaching the ad platforms to chase move-ins instead of cheap clicks.

(Important honesty note, covered fully in [WHERE-THINGS-STAND.md](./WHERE-THINGS-STAND.md): steps 1–5 are built and working. Step 6 — the part that makes the whole pitch true — is the one piece not yet wired together.)

## What a customer actually experiences

If a storage operator signed up, here's what they'd touch:

### Their dashboard (the "Client Portal")
A clean login at storageads.com where the operator sees their campaigns, their leads, their spend, their move-ins, and their performance reports. They can upload their monthly management reports, message you, and manage their settings. This is real and built.

### Automated weekly reports
Every week (or month), they get a polished email with a **PDF attached** showing: how much was spent, how many leads came in, how many move-ins happened, the cost per move-in, the return on investment, and the best/worst performing campaign — with a short written summary generated by AI. This is real and built.

### A custom facility website + landing pages
StorageAds builds a website and dedicated landing pages for the facility — the places ads send people. Built.

### Call tracking
A dedicated phone number forwards to the facility, records the call, and logs which ad the caller came from. The code is fully built; it just needs a Twilio account switched on (a provisioning step, not a building step).

### Google Business Profile management
Automated review requests, posts, and Q&A handling on their Google listing. Built.

## What the machine does behind the scenes (the part customers don't see)

This is where the real engineering lives. Behind that simple dashboard, the software is doing a lot:

### It creates the ads — with AI
You don't hire a designer. StorageAds uses AI (Claude for words and strategy, plus image/video AI like FAL and Runway) to generate ad copy, images, and even video ads, tuned to the facility and the type of customer (a college student vs. a business storing inventory vs. someone going through a divorce — the system knows six different "customer archetypes"). This is Angelo's domain, and it's genuinely built — there's a whole "creative studio" inside the admin console.

### It actually publishes the ads
This surprised even me to confirm: the software makes **real calls to Meta, Google Ads, and TikTok** to create campaigns — budgets, ad sets, the actual ads. (Safety detail: it creates them *paused* with a small default budget, so a human reviews and hits "go" before real money flows.) This is not a mockup.

### It captures and chases every lead
A lead comes in three ways — a web form, the tracked phone number, or a QR code in the office for walk-ins. The system even captures **partial leads** (people who started filling out a form and bailed) and tries to win them back with automated emails at 1 hour, 24 hours, and 72 hours. Then it runs longer "nurture" sequences of emails and texts. All of this sends for real (email through Resend, texts through Twilio).

### It reads the facility's books
Operators upload their monthly reports from their property-management system (the software that runs the storage facility day-to-day — like storEDGE). StorageAds reads those spreadsheets and pulls out occupancy, rent roll, aging, and revenue, then turns them into intelligence: **churn predictions** (which tenants are about to leave), **rate-increase opportunities** (who can absorb a price bump), and NOI (profit) reports.

### It's actually three products in one
There are three separate "front doors":
- **The Client Portal** — what your customers (facility operators) log into.
- **The Admin Console** (the "Operator's Console") — what *you* log into to run the whole business: a sales pipeline/CRM, all the facilities, campaigns, calls, billing, the creative studio, and intelligence dashboards. This is huge — around 40 different screens.
- **The Partner Dashboard** — for resellers and white-label partners who sell StorageAds to *their* customers and earn a revenue share. Also built, with its own API keys, webhooks, and audit logs.

### It has a public API
Other software can plug into StorageAds through a developer API (`/api/v1/...`) with API keys — for facilities, leads, tenants, call logs, and more. This is the kind of thing that makes a company acquisition-worthy.

## The free tool that feeds the whole thing

At the top of the funnel is a **free AI marketing audit** (storageads.com/audit-tool). A prospect answers questions about their facility, and the AI — pulling in real data from Google about their location and even scraping their existing website — generates a scored report card across eight categories, complete with a "here's how much money you're leaving on the table by doing nothing" dollar figure and a 90-day projection.

It's the lead magnet. When someone views their shared audit report three times, the system flags them as a hot lead and nudges them toward booking a call. This is real and built, and it's clever — it gives away genuine value while qualifying buyers.

## How big is this, really?

To put the scale in concrete terms:

- **~94 database tables** — for comparison, a typical small SaaS has 10–20. Ninety-four means this models a genuinely complex business: CRM, billing, multi-tenant partners, an ad-creative engine, PMS financial data, churn/revenue intelligence, Google profile management, referral revenue-sharing, and a public API.
- **~180 API endpoints** — the individual actions the software can take.
- **21 automated background jobs** — things that run on a schedule without anyone clicking anything: send weekly reports, request reviews, process the lead follow-up sequences, score churn, generate profit reports, clean up old data, and more.
- **~125,000 lines of code**, built by **two people in about six weeks.**

That pace is genuinely unusual. The flip side — and it's important — is that *building a feature* and *finishing/hardening a feature* are different things. The breadth is enormous; some of that breadth is deep and polished, and some is built-but-not-yet-connected. The next document, [WHERE-THINGS-STAND.md](./WHERE-THINGS-STAND.md), draws that line honestly.

## The technology, in one paragraph (skip if you don't care)

It's a modern web app built on Next.js (the framework) and React, hosted on Vercel, with a PostgreSQL database (via Neon). It uses Stripe for payments, Clerk for some auth, Resend for email, Twilio for calls/texts, Anthropic's Claude for the AI writing and strategy, and FAL/Runway/Replicate for AI images and video. Sentry watches for errors. It connects out to Meta, Google, TikTok, and Google Business Profile. None of these are exotic choices — they're the standard, respectable building blocks a serious startup would use, which matters if you ever sell the company.
