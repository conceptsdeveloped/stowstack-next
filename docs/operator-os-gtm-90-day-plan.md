# Operator OS — 90-Day GTM Launch Plan

**Status:** Strategic operating plan. Derivative of `docs/operator-os-vision.md` §7.
**Audience:** Blake (founder, GTM owner), Angelo (co-founder, supporting role on platform infrastructure for outbound), any near-term contractor or hire who joins the GTM motion.
**Purpose:** Convert the four GTM channels selected in the vision into a week-by-week operating cadence with an owner, an output, and a measurable signal per work block. The plan covers the 90 days immediately after Phase 1 alpha ships; the first 30 days run in parallel with the Phase 1 build's final weeks.
**Register:** Internal strategic / operating. Operator-voice copy referenced by name; pitch-voice claims referenced by name; this document itself is plain operating language.

---

## Premise

The vision doc selected four GTM channels to run simultaneously:

1. The proactive audit as cold outreach — the sharpest single mechanic.
2. Founder-led podcast guest circuit — borrowed authority, fast.
3. Daily presence in Reddit and operator Facebook groups — slow-build credibility.
4. Industry conferences — speaking-led, small booth at the top two shows.

This plan sequences all four into a 12-week launch cadence (90 days = 13 weeks; the final week is reserved for review and Phase 2 GTM planning). Each week has a primary motion and supporting motions. Each motion has an owner, an output, and a signal.

The audit-as-cold-outreach mechanic is the highest-leverage channel and gets the largest share of operator time across the 90 days. Conferences are the lowest-frequency channel — two events in the 90-day window. Podcasts are weekly. Reddit/FB presence is daily.

The plan assumes Phase 1 alpha is operationally ready by Week 1 (so audits sent in cold outreach can include the platform's own dogfooding numbers from Blake's portfolio in the "what we'd do for you" close).

## Week-by-week cadence

### Weeks 1-4 — Foundation phase

The first month is about generating proof, warming the channels, and getting the first 20 audits out the door cold to learn what converts. Don't try to scale outreach yet — the goal is to find the email subject line + audit framing + follow-up cadence that actually books calls.

#### Week 1

- **Primary motion:** Soft-launch the audit cold outreach. Send 25 audits to a deliberately curated list — operators Blake knows personally, operators who've engaged with the existing audit tool, and 10 cold operators sampled from the top 50 metros.
- **Owner:** Blake.
- **Output:** 25 cold-audit emails sent with personalized first paragraph and the unsolicited audit attached.
- **Signal to watch:** Open rate (target >55%), reply rate (target >10%), audit click rate (target >30% of opens), call book rate (target >2% of total sends).
- **Supporting motions:**
  - First Reddit post in r/selfstorage — long-form "I built a marketing audit tool for storage operators, here's what I'm learning from running it on my own facilities." Helpful first. Link only at the bottom.
  - Submit pitch to first podcast (Inside Self Storage). Email host with the operator-built angle, attach a sample audit on the host's own facility if they own one.
  - Daily 30 minutes of helpful answers in r/selfstorage and operator Facebook groups. No links.

#### Week 2

- **Primary motion:** Audit cold outreach to 50 operators. Variant testing — half get the "I run a few storage facilities" framing, half get the "I built a marketing audit tool" framing.
- **Owner:** Blake.
- **Output:** 50 cold-audit emails sent across two variants.
- **Signal to watch:** Which subject line + opening converts better. Lock the winner by end of week.
- **Supporting motions:**
  - First booked podcast confirmed. Prep — 5 hours of recording prep, including 3 anecdotes from Blake's own operator life and 2 specific platform demo points.
  - Submit pitches to 3 more podcasts.
  - LinkedIn post: "I audited 75 storage facilities this month. Here's the pattern." Drives audit-tool traffic and qualifies inbound.
  - Daily Reddit/FB presence.

#### Week 3

- **Primary motion:** Audit cold outreach to 100 operators with the winning variant. Begin sending audit reminders to non-respondents from Week 1.
- **Owner:** Blake.
- **Output:** 100 cold-audit emails sent. 25 reminder emails sent.
- **Signal to watch:** Reminder email reply rate. If high, justify a 3-touch sequence; if low, drop the reminder and reinvest in volume.
- **Supporting motions:**
  - Record first podcast.
  - Submit pitches to 5 more podcasts.
  - LinkedIn post: case study from a Blake-portfolio facility — specific numbers, specific actions, no agency pitch.
  - First post in operator-focused Facebook group. Same playbook as Reddit — helpful first.

#### Week 4

- **Primary motion:** Audit cold outreach to 200 operators. Begin 5-touch sequence per `docs/cold-audit-outbound-sequence.md` (audit cold email, audit reminder, LinkedIn connect, SMS follow-up where opt-in exists, final email).
- **Owner:** Blake.
- **Output:** 200 cold-audit emails sent, 5-touch sequence active for all.
- **Signal to watch:** End-of-month conversion: audits sent → calls booked → plans purchased. Target by end of Month 1: 5 paying customers from cold-audit channel.
- **Supporting motions:**
  - First podcast episode airs. Repurpose on LinkedIn, Reddit, FB groups. Send to the audit-cold-outreach list as a follow-up.
  - Second podcast recorded.
  - Register for SSA Spring Convention (or next regional show in window). Submit speaker proposal.
  - LinkedIn post: 30-day review of the audit cold-outreach motion. Open data — "here's what worked, here's what didn't."

### Weeks 5-8 — Scale phase

By Month 2, the foundation is laid: outreach format is locked, first podcast is out, Reddit/FB presence is warm, first paying customers from cold are in. Goal now is to triple the outbound volume, ship 2-3 podcast appearances, and convert 10-15 more paying customers from the channels.

#### Week 5

- **Primary motion:** Audit cold outreach to 500 operators. Move to an SDR-assisted model for the personalization-light portion of outreach; Blake's personal sends reserved for the warmest 50/week.
- **Owner:** Blake + first SDR contractor or contract assistant.
- **Output:** 500 cold-audit emails sent.
- **Signal to watch:** Reply rate from SDR-assisted vs. Blake-personal. If SDR-assisted drops materially, retest framing or rate-limit the channel.
- **Supporting motions:**
  - Two podcasts recorded this week (one short, one long). Repurpose aggressively.
  - LinkedIn post: "First 5 operators on StorageAds — what we learned in 30 days." With permission, anonymized lift numbers.
  - First operator Facebook group post that drives meaningful audit traffic. Track.

#### Week 6

- **Primary motion:** Audit cold outreach to 750 operators. Continue 5-touch sequence.
- **Owner:** Blake + SDR.
- **Output:** 750 cold-audit emails sent. 30+ calls booked across the rolling outreach.
- **Signal to watch:** Calendar booking rate vs. last week. Should be flat or improving as recognition builds.
- **Supporting motions:**
  - Third podcast aired.
  - First "audit reaction" thread on Reddit — long-form post sharing aggregate audit findings (anonymized) across 50 facilities. Drives audit-tool inbound.
  - Begin SSA Spring Convention prep: booth design, takeaway QR-coded one-pager, audit-running iPad for live demos.

#### Week 7

- **Primary motion:** Audit cold outreach to 1,000 operators.
- **Owner:** Blake + SDR.
- **Output:** 1,000 cold-audit emails sent. Inbound audit volume from podcast + LinkedIn + Reddit at 5x the Week 1 baseline.
- **Signal to watch:** Cold vs. warm audit conversion gap. Warm audits (inbound from content channels) should convert 3-5x better than cold; if not, the content positioning needs work.
- **Supporting motions:**
  - Fourth and fifth podcast appearances recorded.
  - LinkedIn post: pricing-page launch. Explain pricing in operator voice. "Here's what each plan does. Here's why."
  - Operator FB group: helpful long-form on speed-to-lead (the platform's strongest hook).

#### Week 8

- **Primary motion:** Audit cold outreach to 1,250 operators. End-of-month review of 8-week motion.
- **Owner:** Blake + SDR.
- **Output:** 1,250 cold-audit emails sent. Month-2 paying-customer tally compiled.
- **Signal to watch:** Cumulative paying customers from all channels by end of Month 2. Target: 15-20 paying customers cumulative (Month 1 + Month 2).
- **Supporting motions:**
  - First aired podcast appearances drive measurable audit-tool inbound; document conversion rates per podcast.
  - SSA Spring Convention final prep.
  - LinkedIn post: 60-day public review of the launch motion. Open data, honest gaps. Drives trust.

### Weeks 9-12 — Conversion + conference phase

Month 3 leans into industry conferences and refining the warm-channel-to-paid-customer conversion. By now there should be a real flywheel: cold outreach drives warm conversation, podcasts and Reddit drive inbound audits, paying customers refer other operators, and the SSA Convention puts Blake on stage in front of the densest concentration of operators in the calendar year.

#### Week 9

- **Primary motion:** SSA Spring Convention. Blake speaks on the operator-to-founder narrative + delivers a public live-audit demo on stage.
- **Owner:** Blake.
- **Output:** Speaking slot, small booth, 200+ on-site audit signups, 30+ booked follow-up calls.
- **Signal to watch:** Lead quality from the conference — operators who book follow-up vs. operators who just take a swag item.
- **Supporting motions:**
  - SDR maintains cold-audit cadence at 1,250/week.
  - Post-conference podcast push — every recorded podcast pre-conference airs in the window after the conference for the "I just saw Blake at SSA" recall effect.
  - LinkedIn post-conference recap, with the live audit demo screen recording.

#### Week 10

- **Primary motion:** Conference follow-up sequence. Every conference contact gets a personalized post-event audit if they didn't get one on-site, plus an email summarizing the demo and the relevant pricing tier.
- **Owner:** Blake.
- **Output:** 200+ post-conference follow-up audits delivered. 50+ booked calls.
- **Signal to watch:** Conference-to-paying-customer conversion. Conference leads should convert at 3-5x the cold-outreach rate; if not, conference investment is mis-priced.
- **Supporting motions:**
  - SDR maintains cold cadence.
  - Begin second-half podcast recording cycle.
  - First case study published — a Phase 1 alpha customer (with permission) telling their story. Operator voice, real numbers.

#### Week 11

- **Primary motion:** Begin the second outreach push — re-engage Week 1-4 non-respondents with the now-improved audit + the case study + the conference recap. Treat as a fresh cold list with updated proof material.
- **Owner:** Blake + SDR.
- **Output:** 1,500 re-engagement emails sent. Cumulative cold-outreach 90-day total approaches 5,000 operators reached.
- **Signal to watch:** Re-engagement reply rate vs. fresh-cold reply rate. If high, the proof-first approach is working; if low, the initial messaging carries permanent damage.
- **Supporting motions:**
  - Second podcast cycle continues.
  - LinkedIn post: 90-day-out content from a Phase 2 external pilot operator (with permission). Drives audit and pricing-page traffic.

#### Week 12

- **Primary motion:** 90-day GTM review. Lock the winning audit framing, the winning podcast circuit, the winning channels, and the winning sales-conversation script. Document what gets scaled into Phase 2.
- **Owner:** Blake.
- **Output:** 90-day-review document (`docs/gtm-90-day-review.md`) covering conversion rates by channel, customer count by acquisition source, CAC by channel, payback period, and the recommended Year-1 GTM allocation.
- **Signal to watch:** Total paying customer count from GTM channels (target: 35-50 paying customers cumulative across the 90 days).
- **Supporting motions:**
  - Third podcast cycle continues.
  - Final Reddit/FB groups post: 90-day open data dump.
  - LinkedIn post: "Where we ended up after 90 days." Real numbers. Real lessons. The honest review is the marketing.

## Channel allocation across 90 days

| Channel | Time allocation (Blake hours/week) | Volume by Week 12 | Expected customer share |
|---|---|---|---|
| Audit cold outreach | 15 hrs/wk weeks 1-4, then 8 hrs/wk weeks 5-12 with SDR scaling | ~5,000 operators reached | 50-60% |
| Podcast guest circuit | 4 hrs/wk recording + repurposing | 6-8 episodes aired | 15-20% |
| Reddit + Facebook groups | 30 min/day daily presence | 12+ long-form posts | 10-15% |
| Industry conferences | 40 hrs (SSA Convention week) | 1 major show | 10-15% |
| Inbound from audit tool (driven by content) | passive | proportional to all above | overlap |

## Tooling for the GTM motion

- **Outreach:** Apollo or Smartlead for the cold-audit sequence. Resend for the audit-deliverable and follow-up emails.
- **Calendar:** Cal.com (already in use). 5 daily 30-min slots, two time zones.
- **CRM:** the existing internal pipeline (`clients`, `partial_leads`, `lead_status_events` per the existing schema). Tag every lead by `acquisition_source` for the channel-attribution math.
- **LinkedIn:** organic only. No paid LinkedIn ads.
- **Podcast prep:** simple call sheet with 3 anecdotes per appearance, 2 demo highlights, 1 CTA (the audit tool URL).
- **SDR contracting:** part-time contractor at $20-30/hr, 20 hrs/wk starting Week 5. Manages SDR-assisted outbound under Blake's brand from Blake's email. Quality bar: zero links auto-inserted; every email reviewed before send.

## Risk register specific to GTM

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cold audit emails marked as spam, blowing Blake's sending reputation | Medium | Catastrophic | Warm Blake's domain via Smartlead for 14 days pre-launch; rotate sub-domains for the SDR-assisted tier; never use shared IPs for Blake-personal sends |
| Podcast hosts decline because they don't know Blake | Medium | Medium | Lead with the audit on the host's own facility as the opener; cite the operator-built positioning as the angle; offer to record their facility's audit live on the show |
| First case-study customer's lift numbers are disappointing | Medium | High | Lean on the Blake-portfolio numbers from `docs/operator-os-pilot-measurement-plan.md` as the primary proof; case study customers reinforce, not replace |
| SSA Convention pulls more leads than the sales motion can handle | Medium | Medium | Pre-built post-conference automation sequence handles bulk follow-up; only the warmest 30% get a personal Blake call |
| Reddit account flagged for self-promotion | High | Medium | Strict helpful-first discipline; 1 link post per 10 helpful comments; never link to a sales page directly — link to the audit tool only |
| Founder bandwidth caps the channel mix | High | High | The 90-day plan is the bandwidth ceiling, not the ambition. After Day 90, hire either an SDR FTE or a partnerships hire — but not before |

## Decision triggers built into the plan

- If cold-audit reply rate < 5% at end of Week 2 — change the framing or open new lists; do not scale further until the unit reply rate is healthy.
- If conference investment yields < 5 paying customers per show — drop conferences from Year-1 allocation, redirect spend to LinkedIn paid or podcast advertising.
- If podcast inbound audit volume is < 10x the cold outbound conversion rate — invest more in proof artifacts before more podcast volume; the inbound is supposed to be warmer than cold.
- If SDR-assisted outreach hits a wall on quality — Blake-personal sending only, accept lower volume, raise customer ACV via Better/Best mix.

## What this plan does not do

It does not commit to LinkedIn paid ads, Google Ads on brand terms, retargeting on the audit tool, or any paid acquisition for the GTM motion itself — those are deferred to Day 90+ once the organic channels' unit economics are known. It does not commit to a partnerships motion (PMS vendor co-marketing, association sponsorship beyond conferences) — those are Year-1 H2 motions, contingent on Phase 2 customer count. It does not commit to a referral-from-customer mechanic — that's a product feature surfacing after the first 30 customers, where customers have lived with the platform long enough to refer credibly.

## What gets reviewed at Day 90

The 90-day review at Week 12 covers:

- Cumulative paying customer count by channel.
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC) by channel — including Blake's hours valued at fully-loaded cost.
- Customer LTV projection (early signal only; full LTV requires 12+ months of data).
- CAC payback period by channel.
- Channel-specific lessons — what worked in the audit copy, what podcast hooks landed, what Reddit threads converted.
- Recommended Year-1 channel allocation by quarter.
- The first GTM hire's job description (SDR FTE vs. partnerships vs. customer success — the decision is driven by where the gating constraint is at Day 90).

The 90-day review is the input to the Year-1 GTM plan, which is its own document, drafted Week 13.
