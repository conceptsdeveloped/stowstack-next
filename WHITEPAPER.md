**A marketing system built by storage operators, for storage operators.**

## Part 1. The problem we built this for

Pick a 300-unit facility sitting at 88% occupancy. That's 36 empty units. At a $110 average rate, that's about $4,000 a month sitting there. Roughly $48,000 a year. The vacant units aren't free — they cost what they would've earned.

So you do the thing. You google *"self storage marketing"*. You hit four kinds of pitches.

**An agency wants $2,500 a month plus ad spend** to run your Google ads, write some blog posts, and send you a 40-page PowerPoint at the end of every month. You can't tell what changed from the report. The agency's billable hour is the unit they sell. They still bill you in a slow month.

**Storable will roll their marketing module into your PMS** — but only if you're on storEDGE or SiteLink. Switching PMS is a year of pain you don't have time for. And their marketing module is built around what they can see inside their own system; it doesn't sit on top of Meta and Google the way you'd want.

**A pricing-intelligence tool** will tell you what your competitors are charging. Useful when you're setting rates. Doesn't fill the empty units.

**Or you do it yourself.** You learn Facebook Ads Manager at midnight after the gate motor goes out. You set up a Google Ads campaign. Three months later you've spent $3,000 and you can't tell whether the 47 form fills it produced turned into five move-ins or twenty-five. Cost per lead is the number Facebook shows you. Cost per move-in is the number you actually need.

We had this problem at our own facility. We tried all four. We landed on the fifth thing — building the system ourselves.

Here's the part nobody likes to say out loud: the REITs solved this ten years ago. Public Storage, Extra Space, CubeSmart, Life Storage, NSA — they have in-house teams running Meta and Google at industrial scale, with internal dashboards that show cost per move-in by unit type by ZIP code. Their cost of customer acquisition is well below yours because their system is well above yours. They take market share every quarter not because they're cheaper, but because their marketing engine is doing things yours can't.

We built our engine for our own facility. Then we turned it into software. That's StorageAds.

## Part 2. What it does, in three steps

### Step 1 — We plug into your facility

You give us read access to your storEDGE. You tell us your unit mix, your rates, your trade area (the drive-time radius your customers actually come from), and where the empty units are. Takes about twenty minutes. No discovery call. No six-week onboarding. No strategy deck.

What we get out of that twenty minutes:

- Your facility on Google Places, your current website, your Google Business Profile, your reviews, your rating.
- A snapshot of your unit mix, your current rates, your occupancy by unit type, your move-ins and move-outs over the last few months.
- Who's renting near you — facility names, ratings, addresses, what they're charging on the units you're competing for.
- An estimated cost of doing nothing — the dollars-per-month your vacant units are leaving on the table.

That last one is the audit. You get it whether you become a customer or not.

### Step 2 — We turn the ads on

Meta and Google ads aimed at people inside your trade area. They land on a page built for *your* facility — your name, your address, your rates, your unit availability — and they reserve the unit right there. No phone tag. No "let me check and call you back." No walk-in required.

The page is built around your storEDGE reservation flow. When somebody clicks "Reserve Now," they're booking the unit in your system, not ours. The reservation lands in your storEDGE the same as if they'd walked in off the street. Your office staff see it on the same screen they always see.

We track the calls too. The phone number on your landing page is a tracking number that forwards to your real office line. We log who called, where they were, how long the call lasted, and which campaign brought them in. Your staff just answers the phone like normal.

And we track the walk-ins. The QR code we ship for your office desk turns a walk-in tenant into a single tap by your staff — "where'd you hear about us?" — that closes the attribution loop on the 30–40% of move-ins that don't start digital.

### Step 3 — You watch the units fill

One dashboard. The screen tells you four things:

- **What you spent.** Total ad spend this period, by platform, by campaign.
- **What you got.** Reservations, move-ins, total rent locked in.
- **What each move-in cost.** Spend divided by move-ins. The number your checkbook actually cares about.
- **Whether you're filling faster than last month.** Occupancy change versus your baseline, in units and in dollars.

That's it. No engagement rates. No impressions. No reach. No "social listening." If we can't tie a number to a move-in or a dollar, it's not on the screen.

## Part 3. What you don't have to learn

A short list of things our system handles so you don't have to:

- **What a CAPI event is.** (Meta's server-side conversion API. We send your move-ins to Meta so their bidder learns what produces real renters, not just form fillers. You never see the term.)
- **What a click ID is.** (The breadcrumb that ties a specific click to a specific reservation. We track it. You never type it.)
- **What a lookalike audience is.** (We build audiences from your existing tenants so Meta and Google can find more people like them. You don't configure it.)
- **What an ECRI candidate is.** (Existing Customer Rate Increase — a long-tenure tenant paying well below market rate. We flag them. You approve or skip.)
- **What an attribution window is.** (The time between a click and a move-in counts. We pick the right window per platform. You don't tune it.)
- **What an aging bucket is.** (You actually do know this one — it's just delinquency timing. We bucket it the way your state's lien-sale laws work.)
- **How to set up Google Business Profile auto-responses.** (We draft your review replies. You approve or edit. We publish.)
- **How to set up an A/B test on a landing page.** (We run the test. We pick the winner when the math is significant. You see the winner.)

You handle the facility. The system handles the marketing.

## Part 5. How it stacks up against your alternatives

You have four other places you could put your marketing dollars. Here's how to think about each.

### Versus an agency (StoragePug, G5, StorageRankers, the local digital shop)

The agency model is fine for some operators. The agency does the work; you don't think about it. If you've got the budget and the relationship is good, it works.

The catch is what you don't get to keep. The landing pages live on their server. The Google Ads account is under their MCC. The tracking is on their stack. The reporting is in their dashboard. If you switch agencies, you start over — and they know it, which is why switching is rare even when results aren't great.

You're also paying for an agency's overhead. The account manager, the strategy lead, the QA layer, the report-writer. All real people, all real cost, all spread across all the agency's clients. You're paying for *your* facility plus a slice of theirs.

We don't do retainers. The infrastructure stays with you. If you cancel, you cancel.

### Versus Storable's marketing module

Storable is the dominant PMS vendor. Their marketing module ships with their PMS. If you're already on storEDGE or SiteLink and you like everything about Storable, their bundle has a real advantage — one vendor, one bill, one throat to choke.

The catch is the bundle works both ways. The marketing decision is tied to the PMS decision. If you want their marketing, you commit to their PMS. If you'd rather use a PMS that fits your facility differently, the marketing isn't an option.

We work alongside whatever PMS you have. storEDGE is fully integrated today. SiteLink, Yardi Self-Storage, and Easy Storage Solutions are next. We don't make you switch the most important software in your operation to get help with the second most important.

### Versus DIY (Google Ads + a spreadsheet)

If you're running ads yourself and tracking them in Excel, you're already doing more than half your competitors. The honest question is whether your time and the quality of the result are where you want them.

A 300-unit facility at $1,500 a month of ad spend is $18,000 a year out the door. If your DIY system gets you to $400 cost per move-in and our system gets you to $250, that's not a small difference. On 50 move-ins a year it's $7,500. That's the rough math we'd run in a sales call. Yours will be different. The audit gives you the real version.

The DIY operator we most often turn into a customer is the one who already proved to themselves that ads can work — they just hit the ceiling of what they can do at midnight on a phone, and they don't want to be the one explaining a botched ECRI rollout to their accountant in April.

### Versus a pricing-intelligence tool (Adverank, Veritec, in-house spreadsheets)

Different category. Pricing intelligence tells you where to set your rates. We tell you whether your marketing is filling units. You probably need both; we don't replace one with the other. Our ECRI flagging touches the rate side, but it's a recommendation layer on top of your PMS data, not a market-rate-discovery tool.

### Versus a generalist attribution platform (Triple Whale, Northbeam)

These are e-commerce attribution tools. They're sophisticated; they're not for you. They don't know what a move-in is. They don't integrate with storEDGE. They're built to track Shopify purchases, not storage-unit reservations. If somebody pitched you one of these for your facility, they were pitching the wrong tool.

## Part 8. What's not built yet (the honest version)

If we showed you the build list with only the wins, you'd be reading a different document. Here's what's still on the list.

**The move-in loop closure is currently manual.** When storEDGE confirms a move-in, our system logs it. The transition from "reserved" to "moved in" on the lead record is currently a click an operator (or our team) makes. The automation that closes the loop is about 50 lines of code and is the next thing on the build list. Until then, move-in attribution numbers depend on someone clicking the button. We're honest about that.

**PMS coverage is storEDGE-first.** SiteLink, Yardi Self-Storage, and Easy Storage Solutions are the next three in the queue. If you're on one of those, the platform still works — you just upload your PMS reports as files for now, and our team processes them while we build the structured connector for your vendor.

**PDF extraction from reports is manual.** If your PMS exports as PDF, the file uploads to our storage and a human reads it. AI-powered PDF parsing is on the build list; it's a known gap.

**Twilio webhook signature verification is on the list.** Calls flow correctly today; the additional security check (verifying the webhook actually came from Twilio and not a spoofer) is a one-day fix we haven't done yet.

**Test coverage is concentrated on the highest-risk paths.** Auth, the Stripe webhook, the external API. The ad-and-attribution surface is tested by us using it, not by automated suites yet. That'll come as we onboard more facilities and need it to.

**We don't do site selection or feasibility work.** If you're looking at buying a lot for a new build, we're not the right consultant. We start when you have units to fill.

**We don't sell pricing intelligence as a standalone product.** Our ECRI flagging is a recommendation layer on top of your PMS data, not a market-rate-discovery service. If competitor rates and rate-elasticity modeling are what you want, you should still have a tool for that.

None of these gaps are reasons not to plug it in. They're reasons we keep building. The platform is real today. It will be better in six months. It will be better still in eighteen.

## Two short asides

### "Why not just use the agency for one more year?"

Three reasons.

One — the agency's incentives aren't aligned with yours. They get paid whether you fill the units or not. Their retainer doesn't go up when the units fill faster; it doesn't go down when they don't. They'll keep billing until you stop them.

Two — the agency owns the infrastructure. The landing pages, the Google Ads account, the tracking, the reporting. When you switch, you start over. Most operators don't switch even when results are bad, because the cost of starting over is real. That's the moat the agency built around your facility, not a moat around the work.

Three — the agency model is built on hours, ours is built on software. Hours don't compound. Software does. Every month we operate, our system gets better at filling units — more data, sharper benchmarks, tuned defaults. Your agency in month 24 is the same agency you hired in month one. The system you plug in is better in month 24 than it was in month one.

### "What about when storEDGE expands their marketing module?"

They probably will. Storable knows the same thing we know — that marketing data and PMS data want to be in one place. If they expand their module to do everything we do, the operator's question becomes: bundle with the PMS vendor, or use the operator-built alternative.

We're betting some operators will want the unbundled version for the same reason they wanted unbundled banking and unbundled phone service. The PMS decision and the marketing decision shouldn't have to be the same decision. If you want to switch PMS without losing your marketing, or change marketing systems without disrupting your PMS, the bundle is the wrong shape. We're the unbundled shape.

That's a bet. We could be wrong about it. If we are, we'll still be a useful tool for the operators who picked us because the operator-built defaults and the cost-per-move-in math are the parts that mattered most to them.
