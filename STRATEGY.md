## I. MARKET ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK

### Reading a Market

Every facility exists in a market defined by these dimensions:

**Population Density**
- Urban (>3,000/sq mi): High competition, high demand. Differentiate on convenience and speed.
- Suburban (1,000-3,000/sq mi): Sweet spot for self-storage. Family-driven demand, moving activity, home overflow.
- Rural (<1,000/sq mi): Limited competition but limited demand. Radius of service matters more — people will drive 15+ minutes if there's no alternative.

**Median Household Income**
- Under $40K: Price sensitivity is extreme. Lead with lowest available price. First-month-free outperforms percentage discounts.
- $40K-$75K: Value-conscious but not price-only. Social proof and convenience matter as much as price.
- Over $75K: Price is less important than convenience, security, and perceived quality. Climate-controlled units, clean facility photos, and premium positioning win.

**Housing Dynamics**
- High rental ratio (>40%): Transient population = higher move-in/move-out frequency. Storage demand tied to residential turnover.
- High homeowner ratio: Storage demand tied to life events (renovation, downsizing, inheritance). Longer tenancy, lower churn.
- New construction activity: Indicates population growth. Early-mover advantage in new developments.

**Age Distribution**
- College-heavy (18-24): Seasonal demand spikes around academic calendar. Small unit focus. Short-term leases.
- Young professional (25-40): Moving frequently. Life transitions (marriage, first home, baby). Medium units.
- Established (40-65): Downsizing, business storage, hobby storage. Larger units, longer tenancy, climate-controlled.
- Retirement (65+): Downsizing is the primary driver. Sensitive to accessibility, ground-floor units, drive-up access.

### Addressable Market Calculation

For any facility:
1. Population within 5-mile radius
2. × Storage penetration rate (national average: ~9.5% of households rent storage)
3. × Average monthly rate
4. = Total addressable monthly revenue in the market
5. ÷ Total units in the market (all competitors + this facility)
6. = Revenue per available unit (RevPAU) — the health metric

## II. FACILITY DIAGNOSTIC MODEL

### Occupancy-Based Strategy Selection

| Occupancy | Strategy | Ad Spend Approach | Primary Goal |
|-----------|----------|-------------------|--------------|
| Below 70% | **AGGRESSIVE ACQUISITION** | Maximum spend. Every channel active. Promotions mandatory. | Fill units at any reasonable cost. |
| 70-80% | **GROWTH MODE** | Strong spend. Focus on highest-converting channels. Targeted promotions on underperforming unit types. | Fill vacant unit types strategically. |
| 80-90% | **SELECTIVE FILL** | Moderate spend. Optimize for cost-per-move-in, not volume. Pull back on promotions for high-demand units. | Fill remaining vacancies without sacrificing rate. |
| 90-95% | **RATE OPTIMIZATION** | Reduced acquisition spend. Shift budget to retention, rate increase communication, and upsell. | Maximize revenue per unit, not unit count. |
| 95%+ | **REVENUE MAXIMIZATION** | Minimal or zero acquisition spend. No promotions. Focus on rate increases, premium unit positioning. | Extract maximum revenue from full facility. |

### Unit Mix Analysis

**Priority fill order:**
1. Units at <80% occupancy with the highest total count (most revenue impact)
2. Units with the widest gap between street rate and web rate (pricing opportunity)
3. Units where actual average rate is significantly below street rate (existing tenants on old pricing)

**Never advertise:**
- Unit types at 100% occupancy
- Unit types being phased out or converted

### Velocity Metrics

- **Move-in velocity**: Move-ins per month. Below 5% of total units = demand problem. Above 15% = either strong growth or high churn.
- **Move-out velocity**: Move-outs per month. Above 10% of total units = retention problem. Investigate rate sensitivity and customer satisfaction.
- **Net absorption**: Move-ins minus move-outs. Negative net absorption for 2+ consecutive months = structural problem requiring strategic intervention.

### Delinquency Signal

- Below 3%: Healthy. Normal operations.
- 3-7%: Monitor. May indicate rate sensitivity in the market.
- Above 7%: Intervention required. Consider lien auction acceleration, payment plan outreach, or rate adjustment for at-risk tenants.

## III. COMPETITIVE POSITIONING

### Competitive Analysis Framework

For each competitor within the 5-mile radius:

1. **Price Position**: Are they above, at, or below our rates? For each unit type.
2. **Google Rating**: Higher rating = harder to out-position. Lower rating = opportunity.
3. **Review Volume**: Facilities with <50 reviews are vulnerable to a review push.
4. **Google Maps Presence**: Photos, posts, Q&A activity, response to reviews. Inactive competitors are vulnerable.
5. **Website Quality**: Slow, outdated, no online reservation = massive opportunity. Our landing pages will outperform.
6. **Ad Activity**: Are they running Meta/Google ads? Check Facebook Ad Library. No ads = market share for the taking.

### Positioning Strategies

- **Undercut**: When our occupancy is below 80% and competitors are above 90%. Take market share with price.
- **Match**: When occupancy and rates are comparable. Win on creative quality, convenience, and conversion speed.
- **Premium**: When our facility has demonstrably better reviews, features, or location. Price above competitors and justify with quality signals.

## IV. CHANNEL STRATEGY

### Channel Selection Decision Tree

**Meta (Facebook/Instagram)**
- Best for: Awareness, lifestyle targeting, reaching people before they actively search
- When to use: Always, unless budget is under $500/mo. Meta is the awareness engine.
- Budget allocation: 40-60% of total digital spend for facilities under 85% occupancy
- Key metric: Cost per move-in, not cost per click

**Google Search**
- Best for: Capturing active intent ("storage units near me")
- When to use: Always. Non-negotiable for any facility spending on digital.
- Budget allocation: 30-50% of total digital spend
- Key metric: Cost per conversion (reservation or call)

**Google Business Profile**
- Best for: Local visibility, trust signals, direct calls
- When to use: Always. Free. No reason not to optimize.
- Activity: Weekly posts, respond to every review within 24 hours, update photos quarterly

**SMS/Twilio**
- Best for: Re-engaging leads who visited but didn't convert, reservation reminders, move-in follow-up
- When to use: Only for warm leads with consent. Never for cold outreach.
- Frequency: Max 2 messages per lead without response. Always include opt-out.

**TikTok (Organic)**
- Best for: Brand awareness, humanizing the facility, reaching younger demographics
- When to use: When the market has significant 18-35 population. Not a direct-response channel — build presence.

## V. AUDIENCE SEGMENTATION

### Storage Customer Archetypes

**The Mover** (30% of storage customers)
- Trigger: Relocating, between homes, temporary situation
- Timeline: Needs storage within 1-2 weeks
- Unit need: Medium to large (10x10, 10x15, 10x20)
- Duration: 2-6 months
- Ad angle: Convenience, speed, "bridge" between homes
- Targeting: Life event (moving), real estate interest, apartment hunting

**The Downsizer** (20%)
- Trigger: Empty nest, retirement, moving to smaller home
- Timeline: Planned, 1-3 months lead time
- Unit need: Medium, often climate-controlled
- Duration: 6-24 months (often longer than planned)
- Ad angle: Peace of mind, preserving memories, organized transition
- Targeting: 50+, homeowner, retirement interest

**The Renovator** (15%)
- Trigger: Home renovation, remodel, construction
- Timeline: 2-4 weeks before project start
- Unit need: Medium to large
- Duration: 1-4 months
- Ad angle: Protect your belongings during renovation
- Targeting: Home improvement interest, contractor services

**The Overflow** (15%)
- Trigger: Too much stuff, garage full, no space
- Timeline: No urgency — needs convincing
- Unit need: Small to medium (5x5, 5x10, 10x10)
- Duration: 12+ months (often becomes permanent)
- Ad angle: Reclaim your space, finally organize
- Targeting: Home organization interest, small home/apartment

**The Business** (10%)
- Trigger: Inventory storage, equipment, records, seasonal stock
- Timeline: Ongoing need
- Unit need: Variable, often needs vehicle access
- Duration: 12+ months
- Ad angle: Professional, secure, accessible
- Targeting: Small business owner, entrepreneur

**The Life Event** (10%)
- Trigger: Divorce, death in family, military deployment, college
- Timeline: Urgent, often within days
- Unit need: Variable
- Duration: 3-12 months
- Ad angle: Sensitive, supportive, simple — "one less thing to worry about"
- Targeting: Careful — avoid targeting the event directly. Target the logistical need.

## VI. SEASONALITY & TIMING

### National Storage Demand Curve

- **January-February**: Low demand. Good time for promotions to fill winter vacancies.
- **March-April**: Demand increasing. Spring cleaning, pre-summer moves. Start ramping ad spend.
- **May-August**: Peak season. Highest demand, highest competition, highest ad costs. Focus on conversion efficiency over volume.
- **September-October**: Secondary peak. College move-in, fall relocations.
- **November-December**: Low demand. Reduce acquisition spend, focus on retention and rate optimization.

### Regional Adjustments

- **College towns**: Demand spikes in August/September (move-in) and May (move-out). Plan campaigns 4-6 weeks before each spike.
- **Military bases**: Demand tied to PCS (Permanent Change of Station) cycles. Peak in summer.
- **Sunbelt/retirement**: Less seasonal variation. Steady year-round demand with slight winter uptick from snowbirds.
- **Northern climates**: Strong seasonality. Frozen pipes and snow reduce winter move-in activity.

### Ad Spend Timing

- Increase spend 3-4 weeks before expected demand increase
- Don't wait for vacancies to appear — by then competitors have already captured the demand
- Reduce spend 2 weeks into low-demand periods, not at the start

## VIII. PRICING PSYCHOLOGY

### Price Presentation in Ads

- **Always show the price.** Hiding price creates friction and attracts tire-kickers. Showing price pre-qualifies.
- **"Starting at $X/mo"** is the most effective format. It anchors low while being truthful.
- **Monthly framing beats daily.** "$49/mo" feels affordable. "$1.63/day" feels like a gimmick.
- **Round numbers for simplicity, odd numbers for value.** $49 feels like a deal. $50 feels like a round number.

### Promotional Strategies

- **First month free**: Strongest conversion driver. Use when occupancy is below 80%. Reduces perceived risk.
- **Percentage off**: "50% off first month" = same as half off but sounds bigger. Use when the dollar amount of "first month free" is small.
- **Rate lock**: "Lock in this rate for 12 months" — effective for price-sensitive markets where competitors do frequent rate increases.
- **No deposit**: Removes friction. Effective for price-sensitive segments.
- **Waived admin fee**: Small incentive but signals customer-friendliness.

### Price Anchoring

When showing unit options, always show a more expensive option first. The 10x20 at $149/mo makes the 10x10 at $89/mo feel like a deal. This is automatic when the landing page pulls from storEDGE — ensure the sort order supports anchoring.

## IX. META ADVERTISING INTELLIGENCE

### How Andromeda Ranks Ads

Meta's Andromeda system determines which ads get shown and at what cost. The ranking formula:

**Total Value = Bid × Estimated Action Rate × User Value**

- **Bid**: What you're willing to pay. Automatic bidding usually outperforms manual.
- **Estimated Action Rate**: How likely the user is to take the desired action. Driven by creative relevance, historical performance, and audience match.
- **User Value**: How positive the experience is for the user. Low negative feedback, high engagement, native-feeling content.

### What This Means for StowStack

1. **Creative quality is the lever.** Better creative = higher estimated action rate = lower costs = more impressions.
2. **Relevance signals matter.** Local references, specific pricing, specific unit types — all increase relevance scoring.
3. **Negative feedback kills delivery.** If people hide or report the ad, delivery tanks. Pre-qualification (showing price in the ad) reduces irrelevant clicks and negative feedback.
4. **Fresh creative wins.** Rotate every 2-3 weeks. Andromeda penalizes creative fatigue.
5. **Conversion data compounds.** The more move-ins we track via CAPI, the better Andromeda learns who converts. Full-funnel attribution is a structural advantage.

### Funnel Architecture

**Awareness → Consideration → Conversion → Retention**

Each stage needs different creative and messaging:

| Stage | Goal | Creative | CTA |
|-------|------|----------|-----|
| Awareness | "Storage exists here" | Facility exterior, lifestyle imagery | Learn More |
| Consideration | "This facility is right for me" | Specific units, pricing, reviews | See Available Units |
| Conversion | "Reserve now" | Price, urgency, unit specifics | Reserve Your Unit |
| Retention | "Stay and refer" | Satisfaction, community, referral incentive | Refer a Friend |

Most storage ads operate only at the conversion stage. The facilities that dominate run full-funnel.
