## 5. SpareFoot / Storage.com / SelfStorage.com (Storable Marketplace)

**Owned by:** Storable (acquisition ~2015–2017).

**Founded:** 2008 (SpareFoot); Austin, TX.

**Model:** Pay-per-rental marketplace and lead aggregator. Consumers search properties on SpareFoot.com, Storage.com, or SelfStorage.com, submit lead inquiries (with phone + email), and Storable matches lead to properties. Operators "win" leads in competitive auction format (bid-based). Operators pay per move-in, not per click or lead.

**Pricing Model (Verified):** Two pricing models:
- OneTime pricing: Operator sets multiplier bid (e.g., 2.5x monthly rent). Example: tenant rent is $130/mo, operator bid is 2.5x, move-in cost = $325. Multiples typically 1.5–3.0x monthly rent.
- Legacy RentPercent: Monthly percentage of rent collected for length of tenant stay (older contracts; being migrated to OneTime).

**Example Economics:** $130/mo unit, 2.0x bid = $260 cost per move-in via SpareFoot. For 5-occupied 10x10s per month, cost = $1,300/mo to SpareFoot alone. Effective CPA 10–50x vs. direct Facebook/Google campaigns.

**Consumer-Side Traction:** 1,000+ leads per day across all three marketplace brands (SpareFoot, Storage.com, SelfStorage.com). Trustpilot: 20,375 reviews, 9.1 rating. A+ BBB. High brand awareness among moving consumers.

**Consumer Complaints (Verified):** $29 admin fee per booking (hidden). Inaccurate pricing listed on platform. Call center agents (out-of-country, aggressive) making promises not honored at facilities. Difficulty contacting facilities directly. Poor customer service (phone hold times 30+ minutes).

**Operator Complaints (Self Storage Talk forum consensus):** Expensive ($1,500–$3,000 per move-in in major metros). Lead quality inconsistent (some duplicate leads, leads for units not available, leads for wrong unit sizes). Out-of-country call centers making inappropriate promises ("We'll hold your rate at $99 for 6 months" when facility policy is no holds). Leads sometimes low-intent (browsers, not renters). Bidding wars inflate prices. Aggressive opt-in to SpareFoot by default (hard to opt out). Operators report ROI declining as more competitors join marketplace.

**Integration:** Deep integration with SiteLink and storEDGE (Storable owns all three). Leads flow directly into PMS. Operator can accept/reject lead via PMS interface. Lead data (move-in date, unit size, etc.) pre-populated.

**Market Trend:** Sophisticated operators (50+ facilities) shifting to direct marketing (StorageAds, agencies, in-house teams). Smaller operators still rely on SpareFoot for 20–30% of move-ins. Mid-market operators using SpareFoot for lease-up (short-term spike demand) but trying to shift to cheaper channels for stabilized occupancy.

**What They Don't Do:** Not a marketing service. Does not run ads on operator's behalf. Does not build landing pages, manage campaigns, provide creative optimization, or offer attribution beyond "which lead converted to move-in." Storable controls all consumer messaging, creative, and user experience.

**Stated Value Prop:** "Access a network of millions of moving customers." "Pre-qualified leads." "No marketing headache."

**Strengths:**

- No marketing work by operator (passive lead generation)
- Pay-only-for-results model (no upfront cost; pay on actual move-in)
