### State Association Events

**Major state conferences in target geographies:**

**Florida Self Storage Association (FSSA)**
- Annual conference + smaller regional meetings
- Heavy attendance from Florida operators and management companies
- Typical timing: Fall (October-November)
- Venue: Varies (Miami, Tampa, Orlando)
- Importance: Florida is top-5 market for self-storage; high operator concentration

**Washington Self Storage Association (WA-SSA)**
- Annual conference in Washington State
- Strong operator community (Seattle, Portland, Spokane markets)
- Typical timing: Spring (April-May)
- Venue: Usually Seattle or Bellevue area
- Importance: West Coast traction point

**California Self Storage Association (CSSA)**
- Largest state market; significant conference
- Multiple events per year (regional + state-level)
- Venues: LA, San Diego, Bay Area
- Importance: Tech-forward operators, high marketing spend already

**Texas Self Storage Association (TSSA)**
- Multiple regional chapters (Dallas, Houston, Austin)
- Conference-style events + monthly Chapter meetings
- Timing: Fall + spring
- Importance: Largest state by units; high competition; operators sophisticated

**Other notable state associations:**
- Arizona, Colorado, Georgia, Illinois, Maryland, Massachusetts, Nevada, New York
- Each typically holds annual conference + regional events
- Attendance: 100-500 depending on state size

**Regional opportunities:**
- Northeast Self Storage Conference (covers NY, PA, NJ, MA, CT)
- Southeast self storage groups (GA, SC, NC, FL, TN)

## Strategic Positioning

### Authority Building (Pillar: "Data-Driven Operator")

**Key narrative:**
- StorageAds founder (Blake) is an active operator (not just a vendor)
- Personal portfolio is beta test ground for all claims
- Operator-first philosophy: "Solving the problem I had"

**Content angles:**
- Blog posts: "What I learned after spending $X on Google Ads for my facility"
- Case studies: Blake's own facilities (before/after attribution)
- Podcast guest narrative: Operator journey to founder

### Competitive Differentiation (vs. Storable, G5, others)

**At conferences:**
- Storable/G5 are "service providers"; StorageAds is "intelligence partner"
- Their value prop: "We manage your ads"
- StorageAds value prop: "We show you what's actually working"
- Positioning: Complementary or replacement depending on operator sophistication

**Speaking angles:**
- Don't bash competitors; instead elevate the conversation: "Here's what the industry is missing"
- Attribution language: "90% of operators don't know which marketing channel actually moves people in"
- Data narrative: "Let's end guessing and start measuring"

### Lead Generation Strategy

**Post-conference follow-up:**
- Attendee email capture (conference list or business cards at booth)
- Segmentation by role (owner, manager, marketing director)
- Drip sequence: Education first (webinar invites, blog posts), sales second (trial offer)

**Post-podcast:**
- Episode link in show notes includes StorageAds CTA
- Transcript link (if available) with CTAs embedded
- Follow-up email to podcast audience (negotiated with host)
