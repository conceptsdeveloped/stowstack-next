# Ecosystem Map: Strategic Partnerships for StorageAds.com

## Overview

This document identifies every potential strategic partner for StorageAds, organized by category. For each partner, we identify the relationship opportunity, value exchange, difficulty to establish, and potential revenue impact.

**Key principle:** Every partner should create mutual value. StorageAds helps the partner serve their customers better. The partner gives StorageAds access to pipeline, credibility, or distribution.

---

## PMS PLATFORMS (Highest Priority)

These platforms are the foundation of StorageAds' integration strategy. Deep PMS partnerships are critical moats.

### 1. storEDGE (Storable)

**Overview:** Market leader in self-storage PMS. 2,000+ facilities using storEDGE. Parent company Storable also owns SiteLink.

**Relationship Opportunity:**
- Become "preferred marketing partner" for storEDGE customers
- Single sign-on integration: storEDGE → StorageAds
- Lead sync: All leads generated through storEDGE automatically flow to StorageAds (with permission)
- Revenue share: Storable gets 20-30% of StorageAds subscription fees

**Value Exchange:**
- **For Storable:** Operators get marketing attribution natively integrated. Storable can market "storEDGE + StorageAds = complete facility management" to prospects. Incremental revenue from revenue share.
- **For StorageAds:** Access to 2,000+ storEDGE customers. Lower CAC (many customers already in ecosystem). Validation from PMS provider.

**Difficulty to Establish:** Medium
- Storable is a business unit within a larger parent company. Multiple stakeholders (product, sales, exec leadership).
- Storable may see StorageAds as competitor or as complementary product. Early positioning is critical.
- Technical integration is straightforward (API-based).

**Potential Revenue Impact:** High
- If 30% of storEDGE customers adopt StorageAds: 600 customers × $1,000/mo avg = $600k/mo ARR
- If revenue share is 25%, Storable earns $150k/mo
- Break-even for Storable at 200 customers

**Timeline:**
- Month 1-2: Identify right stakeholder at Storable (VP Product or VP Marketing)
- Month 2-3: Schedule call, pitch partnership. Focus on mutual benefit, not competition.
- Month 3-6: Negotiate deal terms, set up integration roadmap
- Month 6-12: Technical integration, market jointly to storEDGE customers
- Expected close: Month 6-9

**Owner:** Blake (relationship), Product/Engineering (technical integration)

**Status:** Not yet initiated

## SELF-STORAGE BROKERS & LENDERS (High-Value Referral Channel)

These are the connectors in the storage ecosystem. Brokers and lenders talk to operators daily and can become referral partners.

### 1. Argus Self Storage Advisors

**Overview:** Nationwide self-storage brokerage and consulting firm. 30+ advisors, 1,000+ transactions per year. Primarily advises on acquisitions, divestitures, and valuations.

**Relationship Opportunity:**
- Referral partnership: When Argus advises a new owner-operator on acquisition or operations, mention StorageAds as marketing partner
- White-label: Argus could resell StorageAds to their clients under Argus brand

**Value Exchange:**
- **For Argus:** They want their clients to succeed (better occupancy = better loan performance, better transaction valuations). StorageAds helps.
- **For StorageAds:** Direct pipeline to owner-operators. Referrals from trusted advisor carry credibility.

**Difficulty to Establish:** Low-Medium
- Brokers are relationship-driven. One call to right person can start partnership.
- No complex technical integration needed
- Revenue share or referral fee is simple to negotiate

**Potential Revenue Impact:** High
- If Argus refers 1 customer/month and 50% convert: 6 customers/year × $1,000/mo × 12 = $72k/year ARR
- If referral fee is $200-500 per customer, Argus makes $1,200-3,000/year (low incentive)
- But if revenue share (20% of customer revenue), Argus makes $1,000+/month per customer referred

**Timeline:**
- Month 1: Identify 2-3 key Argus advisors via LinkedIn
- Month 2: Reach out via LinkedIn or email with "I think your clients would benefit from StorageAds"
- Month 2-3: Schedule call with each, discuss partnership terms
- Month 3: Formalize agreement (referral fee or revenue share)
- Expected close: Month 3-4

**Owner:** Blake (relationship building)

**Status:** Not yet initiated. Easy quick win.

## Notes on Approach

1. **Start easy, build momentum** — Begin with Twilio/CallRail (existing relationships), then brokers (easy sales), then PMS platforms (harder but higher impact).

2. **Every partnership should be mutual** — Don't ask for anything one-sided. Each partner should gain clear value.

3. **Personal relationships matter** — Blake should lead most partnership conversations. Operator credibility makes operators listen to Blake; same applies to partners.

4. **Test before formalizing** — Start with informal referral agreements, prove value, then formalize with revenue share or formal integration.

5. **Track partner CAC** — Measure cost per customer acquired from each partner. Kill partnerships that don't deliver.

6. **Network effects compound** — Early partners (brokers, consultants) create credibility that makes later partners (PMS platforms) easier to land.
