# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) — StorageAds

## Summary

StorageAds' ICP is the **pragmatic, results-driven small operator** (2-15 facilities) in secondary/tertiary markets who is currently underestimating the power of trackable marketing attribution and is frustrated with vendor opacity or rising per-move-in costs. They've been burned before, trust other operators over salespeople, and want to see exactly what their marketing spend produces. They're ready to switch if the ROI is clear and the commitment is month-to-month.

---

## Demographics

### Facility Count
- **Sweet Spot:** 2-15 facilities across one or multiple markets
- **Minimum:** 2 facilities ($500K+ portfolio annual revenue to justify marketing spend)
- **Maximum:** 15 facilities (above 15, they likely have dedicated marketing team or need enterprise platform like G5)
- **Not Served Well:** Single-facility operators (too small), 50+ facility REITs (need G5 scale and features)

**Why This Range?**
- 1 facility: Marketing ROI harder to measure in one unit; operator more likely to rely on "how did you hear about us?"
- 2-15 facilities: Large enough to spend $500-3,000/mo per facility on marketing; small enough to benefit from SMB-focused platform
- 16+: Need enterprise analytics, multiple teams, complex attribution across dozens of units; G5 is better fit

### Market Type
- **Primary Markets:** NOT ideal (REIT-dominated, extremely expensive Google CPCs, high media buy required)
  - Examples: Los Angeles, New York, San Francisco, Miami, Dallas (very competitive)
  - Reason: REITs have unlimited budgets and sophisticated media buying; independent operators can't compete on scale alone
- **Secondary Markets:** Ideal (competitive but not REIT-saturated, CPCs $6-11, room for smart bidding)
  - Examples: Denver, Phoenix, Nashville, Charlotte, Austin, Portland
  - Reason: Enough competition to make attribution matter; not enough to be cost-prohibitive
- **Tertiary Markets:** Ideal (less competitive, lower CPCs, regional operators dominate)
  - Examples: Madison, Des Moines, Boise, Springfield, Fargo
  - Reason: Lower CPCs, local relationships matter, attribution directly tied to market share

**Not Ideal:** Hyper-competitive primary markets where only 50+ facility operators break even on Google.

### Operator Type
- **Owner-Operators:** Primary (they understand their own business, control decisions)
- **Small Management Companies:** Secondary (1-20 facilities under management, decision-making within org)
- **Not Ideal:** Individual property managers (limited budget authority), large REITs (need enterprise), franchisees without owner stake

### Annual Revenue (Portfolio-Wide)
- **Minimum:** $500K annual revenue across portfolio
  - Reason: Less than $500K means fewer than ~8 units at typical $60K annual rent per unit; hard to justify dedicated marketing spend
- **Target:** $500K-$5M (sweet spot for owner-operators and small management companies)
- **Maximum:** $10M (above this, likely 20+ facilities; better served by G5 or internal team)

**Example Profiles:**
- 5 facilities × $120K annual rent per facility = $600K annual revenue ✓ (Perfect ICP)
- 12 facilities × $500K annual rent per facility = $6M annual revenue ✓ (Good ICP, on higher end)
- 1 facility × $150K annual rent = $150K annual revenue ✗ (Too small; owner managing alone)
- 50 facilities × $200K annual rent per facility = $10M annual revenue ✗ (Too large; needs enterprise)

### Current Marketing Spend
- **Current Spend Per Facility:** $500-3,000/mo
- **Total Portfolio Spend:** $1,000-$45,000/mo (varies by facility count and market)
- **Budget Source:** Mixed (owner's pocket, facility margin, from operating budget, sometimes not formalized)
- **Visibility into Spend:** Low (operator knows they spend $X/mo but doesn't know cost-per-move-in)

**Insight:** They're already spending on marketing (Google Ads, SpareFoot, local agencies); StorageAds replaces or augments existing spend, not adds to it.

### PMS System
- **Primary Target:** storEDGE (mid-market standard)
- **Secondary Target:** SiteLink (also widely used)
- **Not Served Well:** Operators on legacy or custom PMS with no API or integration capability
- **Disqualifier:** No PMS or "just a spreadsheet" (can't track move-ins programmatically; attribution impossible)

**Validation:** Early conversations should include "What PMS are you using?" — if not storEDGE or SiteLink, technical integration more complex.

## Psychographics

### Decision-Making Values
- **Results-Oriented:** Cares about move-ins and occupancy, not vanity metrics
- **Pragmatic:** Willing to try new tools if ROI is clear; won't gamble on long-term bets
- **Skeptical of Vendors:** Has been burned by at least one (agency, aggregator, or overpriced platform); wants proof before signing anything
- **Data-Driven (Aspiring):** Understands that marketing spend should be measurable; frustrated when it isn't

### Trust Signals
- **Peer Validation:** Trusts other operators' recommendations far more than vendor claims or marketing copy
- **Transparency:** Needs to see how cost-per-move-in is calculated; won't accept opaque reporting
- **Founder Credibility:** More likely to trust platform built by operators than by marketing generalists
- **No Long-Term Commitment:** Month-to-month is minimum viable trust; anything longer feels risky

### Information Sources
- **Industry Publications:** Inside Self-Storage, Self Storage Talk forums, SSA conference materials
- **Peer Networks:** Facebook groups for storage operators, local operator associations, WhatsApp groups
- **Social Proof:** LinkedIn recommendations from other operators, case studies from similar-size operators
- **Direct Referrals:** Operator friend says "This worked for me"; weights 10x more than any marketing campaign

### Time Horizon
- **Decision Speed:** 2-4 weeks if problem is acute (occupancy dropped, competitor opened, contract ending); slower if urgent need not present
- **Buying Process:** Often wants to talk to other users; may request customer references before signing up
- **Implementation Patience:** Willing to invest 2-3 weeks in setup if ROI expected in 30-60 days

### Market Position
- **Market Knowledge:** Intimately familiar with their local market; knows key competitors by name
- **Seasonal Awareness:** Understands seasonal demand curves (Q3 is busy, Q4-Q1 is slow in most markets); plans marketing accordingly
- **Competitive Awareness:** Knows Google CPC trends locally ("CPCs are up 30% this quarter"; "There's a new REIT moving in")
- **Operator Community:** Engaged in industry (attends conferences, reads blogs, talks shop with peers)

## Buying Triggers

These are the moments when operator is most receptive to StorageAds pitch.

### Trigger 1: Occupancy Drop Below 85%
- **Situation:** Facility occupies 85%+ during good times; drops to 75-82% due to new competitor, seasonal downturn, or marketing mismanagement
- **Mindset:** "I need to move units now. Current approach isn't working."
- **Timeline:** Immediate (next 2-4 weeks)
- **Budget:** Freed up from operations (willing to spend to recover occupancy)
- **Pitch Angle:** "Get move-ins tracked. See what's working in your market. Optimize in real-time."

### Trigger 2: New Competitor Opens Nearby
- **Situation:** Major competitor opens within 5-10 miles (new C-Store, REITs, independent operator with ad budget)
- **Mindset:** "I need to out-market them or lose market share."
- **Timeline:** Urgent (next 1-2 weeks)
- **Budget:** Elevated (willing to invest in marketing arms race)
- **Pitch Angle:** "Move faster than they can. Meta + Google = more impressions at lower cost than they're bidding."

### Trigger 3: Current Vendor Contract Ending
- **Situation:** Agency/SpareFoot/platform contract expires; time to renew or switch
- **Mindset:** "Before I re-up, let me see if there's something better."
- **Timeline:** Planned (30-60 days before renewal)
- **Budget:** Already allocated (moving from old vendor to new vendor)
- **Pitch Angle:** "Try StorageAds month-to-month. If it doesn't work, go back. No risk, full control."

### Trigger 4: Recently Acquired Facility
- **Situation:** Operator just bought 1-3 new facilities (adding to existing portfolio)
- **Mindset:** "I need to lease these units up quickly. What's my fastest path to occupancy?"
- **Timeline:** Urgent (next 2-4 weeks; lease-up momentum is critical)
- **Budget:** High (lease-up is highest ROI investment in facility lifecycle)
- **Pitch Angle:** "Lease up faster with targeted ads. Full attribution shows you exactly what's working."

### Trigger 5: Heard Attribution Success from Peer Operator
- **Situation:** Another operator (trusted peer, at conference, in Facebook group) mentions "StorageAds tracked my move-ins exactly; saved me $X/mo"
- **Mindset:** "Wait, you know your cost per move-in exactly? I don't have that."
- **Timeline:** Immediate (next 1-2 weeks; curiosity-driven)
- **Budget:** Exploratory (willing to try at low risk)
- **Pitch Angle:** "See what other operators in your region are seeing. Month-to-month, risk-free."

## Disqualifiers

These operator profiles are NOT good ICP matches. Do not pursue hard; resources better spent on ICP matches.

### Disqualifier 1: Single Facility Under $50K Annual Revenue
- **Why:** Too small to justify dedicated marketing platform; owner doing everything (sales, ops, maintenance); limited budget for experimentation
- **Alternative:** Point to free audit tool as entry point; revisit when they acquire 2nd facility

### Disqualifier 2: REIT or 50+ Facility Portfolio
- **Why:** Needs enterprise-scale analytics, multiple team logins, complex reporting; better served by G5 or RealPage
- **Alternative:** If they express interest, recommend G5; offer to help with eventual SMB facilities they might spin off

### Disqualifier 3: Operator Who Wants to Run Their Own Ads
- **Why:** StorageAds offers managed service + self-serve; some operators want 100% control over creative/bidding
- **Alternative:** Offer self-serve reporting access; not primary use case but don't push too hard

### Disqualifier 4: No PMS or Very Outdated System
- **Why:** Can't track move-ins programmatically; attribution impossible without manual work; high implementation cost
- **Example:** Still using paper files or ancient QuickBooks; would need to upgrade PMS first (6-month process)
- **Alternative:** Suggest they upgrade PMS first; circle back in 6 months

### Disqualifier 5: Facilities at 95%+ Occupancy with No Growth Plans
- **Why:** Marketing ROI diminishing; limited room to move units; operator not motivated to test new channels
- **Mindset:** "Why invest in marketing when we're full?"
- **Alternative:** Plant seed; revisit when they acquire new facility or occupancy naturally drops

## Engagement Strategy by Operator Type

### Owner-Operator, 3-8 Facilities, Secondary Market

**How to Reach:**
- SSA conferences (booth + networking)
- Self Storage Talk forums (thought leadership posts)
- Facebook groups for storage operators
- Direct outreach from peer referral ("Blake's friend runs StorageAds")
- Free audit tool landing page

**Key Messaging:**
- "Stop paying per rental. Know your cost per move-in."
- "Built by an operator. We run facilities too."
- "Month-to-month. Zero contracts."

**Timeline:** 2-4 week sales cycle; wants to talk to other operators first.

**Success Factor:** Credibility of Blake + 1-2 existing customer references (peer operators).

### Small Management Company, 5-15 Facilities

**How to Reach:**
- Direct outreach to marketing/acquisitions lead
- LinkedIn (target VP of Marketing or Acquisitions)
- Industry events (SSA, ARCO, local operator associations)
- Referral from property management peer groups

**Key Messaging:**
- "Standardize marketing across your portfolio. Track what works."
- "One platform for all facilities. Consistent reporting."
- "Lower per-facility cost than agency; full attribution instead of agencies' guesses."

**Timeline:** 3-6 week sales cycle; need buy-in from CFO (cost) and VP of Marketing (integration).

**Success Factor:** Clear ROI case study for multi-facility operator; integration with their PMS.

### Facility Manager / Marketing Manager (Not Owner)

**How to Reach:**
- LinkedIn outreach (emphasize admin/reporting efficiency)
- Industry training events
- Slack groups for facility operations professionals

**Key Messaging:**
- "Reduce manual reporting. Automate move-in tracking."
- "Show your ownership exactly what marketing produces."
- "One dashboard instead of three platforms."

**Timeline:** 4-8 weeks (need to sell upward to ownership/CFO); manager acts as champion.

**Success Factor:** Ease of implementation; clean reporting that makes manager look good internally.

## Conversation Starters & Discovery Questions

### Initial Conversation
1. "How many facilities do you manage, and what PMS are you using?"
   - Filters: <2 facilities ✗, non-storEDGE/SiteLink ✗
2. "What are you currently spending on marketing each month, and how do you track ROI?"
   - Reveals: Budget size, marketing maturity, attribution pain
3. "Have you ever tried [SpareFoot / Google Ads / agency]? What worked or didn't work?"
   - Reveals: Buying history, pain points, openness to trying new approach
4. "If you could change one thing about your current marketing, what would it be?"
   - Reveals: Top pain point (attribution? cost? service? contracts?)

### Deeper Discovery (If Qualified)
5. "Walk me through how a lead becomes a move-in. What data do you capture at each stage?"
   - Reveals: PMS workflow, current attribution process, technical integration readiness
6. "What does your current cost per move-in look like, and does that number feel right to you?"
   - Reveals: Awareness of true cost, frustration level, readiness to optimize
7. "If we could show you exact cost-per-move-in by ad campaign, month-over-month, would that change how you market?"
   - Reveals: Core motivation (ROI accountability); if yes, strong ICP match
